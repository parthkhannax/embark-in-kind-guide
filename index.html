01 Why This Matters to You

You've done everything right. You held through volatility, watched your RSUs vest, stayed loyal to a high-conviction position. Now you're sitting on $500K, $1M, maybe $5M in a single appreciated stock — and you want options.

The problem is simple and painful: selling means a massive tax hit. Federal long-term capital gains rates up to 20%, plus the 3.8% Net Investment Income Tax, plus state taxes. For a California-based executive, that's potentially 37%+ of gains evaporating — before you've even reinvested a dollar.

Scenario

An executive holds $2M in concentrated META stock with a $200K cost basis.

She wants to diversify, generate income, and protect against downside — but selling the position would trigger approximately $360,000+ in federal and state capital gains tax. She needs a way to move, restructure, or unlock value from the actual shares — without converting to cash first. This is where "in kind" comes in, and why solutions like Embark Funds exist.

The concept is straightforward: "in kind" simply means moving the actual asset — not cash. The shares themselves transfer, your cost basis carries over, and no taxable event is triggered. What that unlocks, however, can be transformative.

02 "In Kind" Defined, Plain and Simple

Across finance, "in kind" means transferring the asset itself rather than converting it to cash first. The term comes from the Latin in genere — "in the same form."

The Key Distinction

Cash Transfer

Sell shares → Realize gains → Pay taxes → Move cash → Rebuy shares at new cost basis

In-Kind Transfer

Shares move directly → Cost basis carries over → No taxable event → Position stays intact

Why does this matter practically? Because your cost basis carries over, no taxable event is triggered, and your position stays intact. You maintain the same lot dates, the same holding period, and the same unrealized gain — just in a different account or structure.

Key Takeaway: An in-kind transfer moves the investment without disrupting its tax characteristics. Think of it as changing the envelope without touching the letter inside.

03 Types of In-Kind Transfers

Not all in-kind transfers are the same. Here are the four primary categories:

1

Broker-to-Broker Transfers (ACAT/ACATS)

The most common type. Moving your portfolio from one brokerage to another using the Automated Customer Account Transfer Service. Your stocks, ETFs, bonds, and options move electronically without liquidation.

2

IRA-to-IRA Rollovers

Transferring retirement account holdings between custodians. Securities move directly — no distribution, no tax withholding, no 60-day rollover window risk.

3

In-Kind Contributions into Funds or Structured Vehicles

Contributing actual shares into exchange funds, SPVs, or similar investment vehicles. Under Section 351 of the tax code, this can be done without triggering a taxable event — the basis of the contributed stock carries over into the new entity. This is the mechanism Embark Funds uses.

4

In-Kind Distributions

Receiving actual securities instead of cash from a fund, trust, or estate. Common in inherited portfolios or when redeeming from certain pooled investment vehicles.

04 The ACAT Process: How It Actually Works

The Automated Customer Account Transfer Service (ACATS) is the industry-standard system for moving assets between brokerages. Here's what actually happens:

1

Open the Receiving Account

Set up your new brokerage account. Ensure the account type matches — taxable to taxable, IRA to IRA.

2

Submit Transfer Initiation Form (TIF)

The receiving broker initiates the request. You'll provide your sending account number, firm name, and specify full or partial transfer.

3

Brokers Coordinate via ACATS

The sending and receiving firms validate your holdings, reconcile positions, and handle the electronic transfer through NSCC's system.

4

Assets Land in Your New Account

Typically 3–6 business days. Same shares, same cost basis, same holding periods — just a new custodian.

What Transfers Cleanly — and What Doesn't

Transfers Smoothly

  • → Individual stocks & ETFs
  • → Corporate & government bonds
  • → Standard mutual funds
  • → Options contracts
  • → Treasury securities

May Not Transfer

  • → Proprietary mutual funds
  • → Cryptocurrency
  • → Certain CDs with early withdrawal penalties
  • → Annuities held at the sending institution
  • → Limited partnerships with transfer restrictions

Common Pitfalls: Pending trades, active DRIPs (Dividend Reinvestment Plans), and margin balances can delay or block your transfer. Settle all open trades and disable automatic features before initiating.

05 Tax Implications: The Real Reason People Transfer In Kind

This is the section that matters most. The entire value proposition of an in-kind transfer — whether it's a simple ACAT or a contribution into a structured vehicle — comes down to one principle:

No capital gains are triggered at the point of transfer.

Your cost basis and holding period carry over to the new account or entity. The IRS treats it as if nothing changed — because, in an economic sense, it didn't. You still own the same position.

Section 351 Exchanges

When contributing appreciated stock into a fund or SPV, Section 351 of the Internal Revenue Code allows for tax-free transfers — provided the contributor(s) collectively control 80% or more of the receiving entity. This is the legal framework that makes in-kind contributions to exchange funds and vehicles like Embark's SPVs possible without triggering recognition of gains.

This mechanism is why Embark's in-kind contribution model is so powerful for concentrated stock holders: you contribute shares, defer taxes, and immediately begin earning income on the full pre-tax value of your position.

Holders of pre-IPO or post-IPO stock looking to monetize their equity without selling their shares or triggering a taxable event.

09 Practical Checklist: Before You Initiate

Whether you're moving assets between brokers or contributing into a structured vehicle, run through this list before you start:

Confirm asset eligibility

Verify that both the sending and receiving institutions support in-kind transfer of your specific securities.

Disable automatic features

Turn off DRIP (Dividend Reinvestment Plans), recurring investments, and auto-rebalancing before initiating.

Settle open trades and margin

Close all pending trades and resolve any margin balances or obligations.

Verify account types match

IRA to IRA, taxable to taxable. Mismatched account types can trigger unintended tax consequences.

Document cost basis and holding periods

Screenshot or export your lot-level cost basis data for your records. Don't rely solely on the receiving broker to get it right.

Consult a tax advisor for fund contributions

If you're contributing into a structured vehicle (exchange fund, SPV), work with a qualified tax professional to ensure compliance with Section 351 and applicable regulations.
